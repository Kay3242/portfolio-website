This was my first 3D model created in Blender, following the classic "doughnut tutorial" to understand foundational tools in sculpting, mesh editing, and modifiers. The project introduced me to the full 3D workflow—from primitive shapes to organic form manipulation—within a playful and low-pressure context.

The doughnut's frosting, sprinkles, and surface properties were developed using Blender's material editor and shader nodes. I experimented with glossy, subsurface, and bump-mapped textures to simulate soft pastry and shiny sugar elements. Adjusting materials helped me grasp how light interacts with different surfaces in realistic rendering.

In the final scene, I arranged multiple doughnuts and used area lights, depth of field, and reflection planes to create a warm, appetizing render. This stage taught me the impact of environmental lighting and camera framing in conveying realism and mood—key skills I now carry into more advanced modeling projects.
